# SOP structure (reference)

A reusable skeleton for a research Statement of Purpose. This is scaffolding —
the actual drafting, scoring, and iteration is done by `apply-sop-writer`. Tailor
the fit paragraph(s) per school; reuse the rest.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph skeleton (~1–2 pages)

1. **Hook + research focus (1 short para).** What question/area drives you and a
   concrete signal of why. Avoid childhood-origin clichés; open with intellectual
   substance.
2. **Research experience #1 (1 para).** A specific project: the problem, *your*
   contribution, the method, the result, and what you learned. Show research
   maturity, not a task list.
3. **Research experience #2 (1 para).** A second project or a deeper dive,
   ideally showing growth or range. Emphasise ownership and outcomes.
4. **Future research direction (1 para).** What you want to work on in the PhD —
   specific enough to be credible, open enough to evolve. Connect it to the arc
   of paras 2–3.
5. **Program fit (1 para, tailored per school).** Name 2–3 faculty whose *recent*
   work aligns, with specifics (`apply-research-direction-mapper`,
   `apply-program-fit-mapper`). Why *this* department, beyond ranking.
6. **Close (1–2 sentences).** Brief, forward-looking; restate the fit.

## Principles

- **Show, don't claim.** "I am passionate about ML" is worthless; a paragraph
  demonstrating a hard problem you pushed through is not.
- **Research > coursework.** Grades belong on the transcript; the SOP is about
  research ability and direction.
- **Fit is specific.** Generic praise of a "world-class program" reads as a form
  letter. Cite actual labs/papers.
- **Consistency.** The SOP must agree with the CV and the recommenders' likely
  framing.

Run `apply-sop-writer` to draft, then score and iterate; run
`apply-package-auditor` to check the SOP against the rest of the package.
