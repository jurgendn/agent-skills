# IELTS Band Descriptors (reference)

Shared scoring reference for the IELTS skills (`ielts-writing-task1`,
`ielts-writing-task2`, `ielts-speaking-coach`, and `ielts-learning-planner`).
Read the relevant section **before** assigning a band so scoring stays anchored
to the public criteria rather than to a remembered impression.

> **Status & attribution.** This is a faithful *paraphrase* of the publicly
> released IELTS band descriptors, condensed to bands 5–9 (the range most
> candidates work in). It is a working aid, **not** the official wording. For
> the verbatim descriptors and bands below 5, use the official sources — they
> are the authority if anything here disagrees:
> - Writing (Task 1 & Task 2): <https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/writing-band-descriptors-task-1.ashx> and <https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/writing-band-descriptors-task-2.ashx>
> - British Council combined Writing descriptors: <https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_writing_band_descriptors.pdf>
> - Speaking: <https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/speaking-band-descriptors.ashx>
> - Overview of criteria: <https://ielts.org/news-and-insights/ielts-writing-band-descriptors-and-key-assessment-criteria>

Each task is scored on **four equally weighted criteria (25% each)**; the four
band scores are averaged (Writing Task 2 is weighted roughly twice Task 1 in the
overall Writing band). Half bands are awarded.

## Writing Task 1 (Academic report / GT letter)

Criteria: **Task Achievement · Coherence & Cohesion · Lexical Resource · Grammatical Range & Accuracy**

### Task Achievement
- **9** — Fully satisfies all requirements; presents a clear, fully developed overview/response with well-chosen, accurately reported key features.
- **8** — Covers all requirements sufficiently; key features clearly presented, highlighted, and illustrated; minor omissions only.
- **7** — Covers the requirements; clear overview of main trends/stages; key features highlighted but could be more fully or precisely extended.
- **6** — Addresses the requirements; an overview is present but features may be inadequately covered; some irrelevant, inaccurate, or mechanical detail.
- **5** — Generally addresses the task but format may be inappropriate; mechanical recounting with no clear overview; detail may be irrelevant, missing, or inaccurate.

### Coherence & Cohesion
- **9** — Cohesion attracts no attention; paragraphing is skilful.
- **8** — Information and ideas managed logically; cohesion well managed; rare lapses.
- **7** — Logical organisation with clear progression; a range of cohesive devices used well, though with some under/over-use; paragraphing may be faulty.
- **6** — Coherent overall; cohesive devices used but sometimes inaccurate, mechanical, or over-used; referencing not always clear.
- **5** — Some organisation but no clear progression; cohesive devices inadequate, inaccurate, or over-used; repetitive due to weak referencing/substitution.

### Lexical Resource
- **9** — Wide range used naturally and precisely; very rare minor slips.
- **8** — Wide range fluently and flexibly; skilful with less common items; occasional inaccuracy in word choice/collocation.
- **7** — Sufficient range for flexibility and precision; some less common vocabulary and awareness of style/collocation; occasional errors in word choice/spelling that don't impede.
- **6** — Adequate range for the task; meaning clear despite some imprecise word choice; some errors in spelling/word formation that don't impede communication.
- **5** — Limited but minimally adequate range; noticeable errors in spelling/word formation that may cause some difficulty for the reader.

### Grammatical Range & Accuracy
- **9** — Wide range used fully flexibly and accurately; very rare minor slips.
- **8** — Wide range; majority of sentences error-free; occasional non-systematic errors/inappropriacies.
- **7** — A variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences; good control with a few errors.
- **6** — Mix of simple and complex forms; some errors in grammar/punctuation but they rarely reduce communication.
- **5** — Limited range of structures; attempts complex sentences but they are less accurate than simple ones; frequent errors and faulty punctuation that may cause some difficulty.

## Writing Task 2 (essay)

Same criteria as Task 1 **except** Task Achievement becomes **Task Response** (the essay must answer the question fully and sustain a clear position). Coherence & Cohesion, Lexical Resource, and Grammatical Range & Accuracy use the same band logic as above.

### Task Response
- **9** — Fully addresses all parts of the task; presents a fully developed position with relevant, well-supported, well-extended ideas.
- **8** — Sufficiently addresses all parts; a well-developed response with relevant, extended, supported ideas; minor under-development only.
- **7** — Addresses all parts though some more fully than others; presents a clear position throughout; ideas extended and supported, but may over-generalise or lack focus in places.
- **6** — Addresses all parts though some more than others; a relevant position, though conclusions may be unclear or repetitive; ideas relevant but some inadequately developed or unclear.
- **5** — Addresses the task only partially; position expressed but development unclear and no clear conclusions; some ideas under-developed or irrelevant.

> Quick anchors: **7** = position clear throughout + all parts addressed; **6** = position present but development uneven; **5** = partial answer / unclear position. The same one-band-up/down logic applies to the other three criteria using the Task 1 wording above.

## Speaking

Criteria: **Fluency & Coherence · Lexical Resource · Grammatical Range & Accuracy · Pronunciation**

### Fluency & Coherence
- **9** — Speaks fluently with only rare repetition/self-correction; hesitation is content-related, not language-search; coherent, fully appropriate cohesion.
- **8** — Fluent with only occasional repetition/self-correction; hesitation usually content-related; develops topics coherently and appropriately.
- **7** — Speaks at length without noticeable effort; some language-related hesitation or loss of coherence; flexible use of connectives/discourse markers.
- **6** — Willing to speak at length, though coherence may be lost through repetition, self-correction, or hesitation; uses a range of connectives but not always appropriately.
- **5** — Usually maintains flow but relies on repetition/self-correction/slow speech; may over-use certain connectives; produces simple speech fluently but loses fluency in complex communication.

### Lexical Resource
- **9** — Full flexibility and precise use in all contexts; idiomatic language used naturally and accurately.
- **8** — Wide resource used readily and flexibly to convey precise meaning; effective use of less common and idiomatic items with occasional inaccuracy; effective paraphrase.
- **7** — Flexible vocabulary to discuss a variety of topics; some less common and idiomatic items with some awareness of style/collocation; effective paraphrase as needed.
- **6** — Wide enough to discuss topics at length and make meaning clear despite inappropriacies; generally able to paraphrase successfully.
- **5** — Manages to talk about familiar and unfamiliar topics but uses vocabulary with limited flexibility; attempts paraphrase with mixed success.

### Grammatical Range & Accuracy
- **9** — Full range used naturally and accurately; very rare minor slips like those of a native speaker.
- **8** — Wide range of structures flexibly; majority error-free; occasional inappropriacies/basic non-systematic errors.
- **7** — A range of complex structures with some flexibility; frequent error-free sentences though some grammatical mistakes persist.
- **6** — A mix of short and complex forms but with limited flexibility; frequent errors in complex structures, though these rarely impede communication.
- **5** — Basic forms with reasonable accuracy; limited range of complex structures, usually with errors that may cause some difficulty.

### Pronunciation
- **9** — Full range of features used precisely and subtly; effortless to understand.
- **8** — Wide range of features; sustains flexible use with only occasional lapses; easy to understand, L1 accent has minimal effect.
- **7** — Shows all the positive features of band 6 and some, but not all, of band 8; generally easy to understand throughout.
- **6** — A range of features with mixed control; some effective use but not sustained; generally understood throughout, though mispronunciation of individual words/sounds reduces clarity at times.
- **5** — Shows all the positive features of band 4 and some of band 6; can generally be understood but mispronunciation causes lapses.

## How to score (apply consistently)

1. Score **each of the four criteria independently** — don't let one strong dimension inflate the others.
2. For a criterion, find the band whose description the response **best fits as a whole**; if it sits between two, award the half band.
3. Average the four (round to the nearest half band). State the four sub-scores, not just the overall — the breakdown is where actionable feedback lives.
4. Tie every band you give to **specific evidence** in the response (quote the phrase, the error, the missing overview), then name the single highest-leverage move to the next band.
