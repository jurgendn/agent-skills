# IELTS Band Descriptors — Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA)

Scoped to the **GRA criterion only** (this skill isolates GRA; for a whole-task band
across all four criteria use `ielts-writing-task2` / `ielts-speaking-coach`). GRA is
one of four equally-weighted criteria; the task band is the average of all four,
rounded to the nearest 0.5. Consult this before assigning a GRA band so scoring stays
anchored to the public criteria, not impression.

Official public descriptors:
- Writing Task 2: https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/writing-band-descriptors-task-2.ashx
- Writing Task 1: https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/writing-band-descriptors-task-1.ashx
- Speaking:       https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/speaking-band-descriptors.ashx

GRA turns on the balance of two things: **range** (variety of structures attempted)
and **accuracy** (how often whole sentences are error-free). Neither alone is enough —
all-simple-but-correct is capped, and complex-but-error-ridden is capped.

---

## Writing GRA (Task 1 & Task 2)

| Band | Descriptor |
|------|-----------|
| 9 | Uses a wide range of structures with full flexibility and control; produces rare errors only as slips |
| 8 | Uses a wide range of structures; the majority of sentences are error-free; makes only very occasional errors or inappropriacies |
| 7 | Uses a variety of complex structures; produces frequent error-free sentences; has good control of grammar and punctuation but may make a few errors |
| 6 | Uses a mix of simple and complex sentence forms; makes some errors in grammar and punctuation but they rarely reduce communication |
| 5 | Uses only a limited range of structures; attempts complex sentences but these tend to be less accurate than simple sentences; may make frequent grammatical errors and punctuation may be faulty; errors can cause some difficulty for the reader |
| 4 | Uses only a very limited range of structures with only rare use of subordinate clauses; some structures are accurate but errors predominate, and punctuation is often faulty |
| 3 | Attempts sentence forms but errors in grammar and punctuation predominate and distort the meaning |

## Speaking GRA

| Band | Descriptor |
|------|-----------|
| 9 | Uses a full range of structures naturally and appropriately; produces consistently accurate structures apart from slips characteristic of native-speaker speech |
| 8 | Uses a wide range of structures flexibly; produces a majority of error-free sentences with only very occasional inappropriacies or basic/non-systematic errors |
| 7 | Uses a range of complex structures with some flexibility; frequently produces error-free sentences, though some grammatical mistakes persist |
| 6 | Uses a mix of simple and complex structures but with limited flexibility; may make frequent mistakes with complex structures, though these rarely cause comprehension problems |
| 5 | Produces basic sentence forms with reasonable accuracy; uses a limited range of more complex structures, but these usually contain errors and may cause some comprehension problems |
| 4 | Produces basic sentence forms and some correct simple sentences but subordinate structures are rare; errors are frequent and may lead to misunderstanding |
| 3 | Attempts basic sentence forms but with limited success, or relies on apparently memorised utterances; makes numerous errors except in memorised expressions |

Writing and Speaking GRA wording differs slightly but the logic is identical: range ×
accuracy.

---

## Scoring heuristics

- **The 6→7 line is error-free-sentence density.** Band 6 = complex attempts but
  *frequent* errors in them; Band 7 = *frequent error-free* sentences among the
  complex ones. Moving 6→7 is almost always "make the complex sentences *correct*,"
  not "add more complexity."
- **The 7→8 line is systematic vs. slip.** Band 7 = "some mistakes persist"; Band 8 =
  errors are "very occasional" and non-systematic. If the same error type recurs
  (e.g. articles, 3rd-person -s), it's systematic → caps at 7. Slips at the margin →
  8. This is why the error ledger matters for scoring, not just coaching.
- **Range gate.** A script with *no* complex structures (no subordination,
  conditionals, relatives, passives) cannot exceed Band 5–6 on range, however
  accurate. Note missing structures explicitly in the "Range" line.
- **Don't over-penalize slips or reward complexity bought with errors.** Weigh how
  often *whole sentences* are error-free, in line with the descriptors above.
