# IELTS Vocabulary Builder

Vocabulary is assessed in Writing (Lexical Resource) and Speaking (Lexical Resource) and is implicitly tested in Reading (understanding paraphrased vocabulary). Band 7+ requires less common, precise vocabulary used accurately; Band 8+ requires idiomatic or sophisticated control with only rare errors.

## Use this when

- The user wants vocabulary for a specific IELTS topic before writing or speaking.
- The user wants to replace repetitive or basic words in their essay or spoken answer.
- The user asks for collocations, word families, or synonyms for a concept.
- The user wants to understand register differences (informal vs. academic vs. journalistic).
- The user wants to practise a topic's vocabulary through gap-fill, example sentences, or recall drills.

## Do not use this when

- The user needs full essay or speaking feedback — use `ielts-writing-task2` or `ielts-speaking-coach`.
- The user is asking about grammar structures — vocabulary and grammar are separate axes on the band scale.

## How Vocabulary is Assessed

### Writing — Lexical Resource (LR)

| Band | What it looks like |
|------|-------------------|
| 9 | Natural, sophisticated control; rare slips only; wide range |
| 8 | Wide range fluently used; uncommon items used skillfully; rare spelling/word-form errors |
| 7 | Sufficient range for flexibility and precision; less common items with awareness of collocation; occasional errors |
| 6 | Adequate range; attempts less common vocabulary with some inaccuracy; some spelling/word-form errors but meaning clear |
| 5 | Limited but minimally adequate; noticeable errors may cause difficulty |
| 4 | Basic vocabulary only; repetitive or inappropriate; errors cause strain |

### Speaking — Lexical Resource (LR)

| Band | What it looks like |
|------|-------------------|
| 9 | Full flexibility and precision across all topics; idiomatic language natural and accurate |
| 8 | Wide vocabulary readily used; less common and idiomatic items with some style awareness; effective paraphrase |
| 7 | Flexible use across topics; less common and idiomatic items; effective paraphrase |
| 6 | Wide enough to discuss topics at length; meaning clear despite some inappropriacies; generally paraphrases successfully |
| 5 | Limited flexibility; attempts paraphrase with mixed success |
| 4 | Basic meaning on familiar topics only; frequent word choice errors; rarely paraphrases |

## Core Principles

### 1. Collocation over isolated synonyms

Do not swap single words without checking the whole phrase. Wrong collocations lower the LR score even if each word exists in English.

| Weak (wrong collocation) | Strong (correct collocation) |
|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| make a crime | commit a crime |
| strong solution | effective solution |
| big importance | great importance |
| do a decision | make a decision |
| rise up | increase / rise |

### 2. Word families

Knowing the noun, verb, adjective, and adverb forms of a word gives range and helps with sentence variety.

| Noun | Verb | Adjective | Adverb |
|------|------|-----------|--------|
| environment | — | environmental | environmentally |
| innovation | innovate | innovative | innovatively |
| significance | signify | significant | significantly |
| employment | employ | employed / unemployed | — |
| education | educate | educational | educationally |

### 3. Register awareness

IELTS Writing requires formal/academic register. Speaking allows semi-formal to formal.

| Informal | Formal / Academic |
|----------|------------------|
| get better | improve / recover |
| go up | increase / rise / surge |
| kids | children / young people |
| a lot of | a considerable number of / numerous |
| really important | crucial / significant / vital |
| find out | discover / identify / determine |

### Environment and Climate

**Key nouns:** greenhouse gas emissions, carbon footprint, biodiversity, deforestation, renewable energy, fossil fuels, climate change, global warming, habitat destruction, ecological balance, sustainability, pollution, conservation

**Key verbs:** mitigate, exacerbate, deplete, conserve, offset, emit, generate (energy), combat (climate change), threaten (species)

**Key adjectives:** sustainable, renewable, irreversible, catastrophic, biodegradable, carbon-neutral, eco-friendly

**Useful phrases:**
- *poses a serious threat to biodiversity*
- *contributes significantly to greenhouse gas emissions*
- *transition to renewable energy sources*

## Academic Word List (AWL) — High-Value Items for IELTS

These appear frequently in Academic Reading passages and are expected in Band 7+ writing.

| Word | Meaning | Example use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| albeit | although | *This is effective, albeit expensive.* |
| apparent | obvious/seeming | *The apparent benefits may mask hidden costs.* |
| consequently | as a result | *Deforestation is accelerating; consequently, biodiversity is declining.* |
| constitute | make up / form | *Young people constitute the majority of the workforce.* |
| evident | clear | *It is evident that inequality has widened.* |
| facilitate | make easier | *Technology facilitates communication across borders.* |
| furthermore | in addition | *Furthermore, governments must take decisive action.* |
| hence | therefore | *Hence, stricter regulations are required.* |
| inevitable | unavoidable | *Technological disruption is inevitable.* |
| inherent | fundamental/built-in | *There are inherent risks in any new policy.* |
| nonetheless | nevertheless | *Nonetheless, progress has been made.* |
| notion | idea/concept | *The notion that growth is always beneficial is flawed.* |
| predominantly | mainly | *The region is predominantly agricultural.* |
| proportion | part/share | *A growing proportion of the population lives in cities.* |
| tend to | usually do | *Young people tend to adopt new technology faster.* |
| whereas | while/in contrast | *Developed nations waste food, whereas poorer nations face shortages.* |

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Identify the need

Determine:
- Topic: which IELTS theme does the user need vocabulary for?
- Skill: is this for Writing, Speaking, or Reading comprehension?
- Level: is the user trying to move from Band 5→6, 6→7, or 7→8?

### Step 2 — Deliver vocabulary in context

Always present vocabulary:
1. In a phrase or collocation, not in isolation.
2. With a model sentence showing real IELTS-style use.
3. With a note on register if relevant (e.g., "too informal for Task 2 writing").

### Step 3 — Drill if the user wants practice

Offer one of three drill formats:
- **Gap-fill:** provide a sentence with a blank; user fills in the correct word/phrase.
- **Replacement:** provide a sentence with a basic word underlined; user replaces it with a more precise alternative.
- **Free production:** give a topic and ask the user to write 2–3 sentences using target vocabulary.

### Step 4 — Feedback

When the user uses vocabulary:
- Confirm correct collocations.
- Flag incorrect collocations and provide the correct form.
- Note register mismatches (e.g., "get worse" → "deteriorate" for formal writing).
- Suggest one or two higher-band alternatives if the user is aiming for Band 7+.

---

## Common Mistakes to Flag

1. **Wrong collocation** — the most common LR error; always check the full phrase, not just the word.
2. **Over-using the same word** — IELTS rewards range; vary across synonyms and word forms.
3. **Informal vocabulary in Task 2** — "kids", "lots of", "really big" drag the LR score down.
4. **Forcing uncommon vocabulary incorrectly** — an inaccurate advanced word is worse than an accurate simple word.
5. **Spelling errors on high-frequency words** — "goverment", "enviroment", "developement" are common and penalised.
6. **Noun/verb/adjective confusion** — "It has environment damage" (wrong form) vs. "It causes environmental damage".

---

## Output format

When delivering a topic vocabulary set:
- Group by part of speech or by function (problem words / solution words / evaluation words).
- Provide a short example sentence for each key item.
- Highlight 3–5 items the user should prioritise for their current band target.

When drilling:
- Provide questions clearly numbered.
- After the user answers, give the answer key with brief explanations for any errors.

When reviewing the user's writing or speaking for vocabulary:
- Quote their text.
- Identify weak or repetitive words.
- Provide a Band 7-equivalent and a Band 8-equivalent replacement where possible.
