# Rubric descriptors — 1 / 3 / 5 anchors per dimension

This file makes scoring **reproducible**: every dimension score must trace to one
of these behavioral anchor bands plus concrete evidence from the dossier. Score
from the anchors, not from gut feel. The descriptor *style* follows published
holistic admissions rubrics (Physics: arxiv.org/abs/2110.04329; ASEE engineering
holistic PhD rubric on peer.asee.org), which use observable behaviors rather than
adjectives.

> **Volatility note.** Anchors describe *kinds of evidence*, never cycle-specific
> numbers (no GPA cutoffs, score minimums, word/page limits, deadlines, funding
> amounts). Where a threshold matters, verify it against the live official call —
> see `external-programs.md` and `degree-equivalence.md`.

## How to score

- **1, 3, 5 are defined.** Use **2** and **4** for clearly-between cases.
- Match the *strongest consistent* evidence, then discount for gaps or overclaim.
- Several dimensions have a **different bar per target type** — A. Research PhD,
  B. Research/coursework Master's, C. Scholarship/Fellowship, D. Research
  internship/pre-doc/RA. Per-type lines appear under each dimension when the bar
  differs; otherwise the anchor is type-agnostic.
- Record, per score: the band, the evidence, and (where relevant) the target type.

## 1. Eligibility & degree equivalence (GATE)

This dimension is a **gate**, not a weighted term. A hard fail caps the dossier:
report "ineligible as-is" + remediation path rather than a competitive score.
For EU / cross-border targets, work the logic in `degree-equivalence.md`.

- **5 — Clearly eligible.** Prior degree(s), credits, and prerequisites
  unambiguously satisfy the target's stated entry rules; any cross-border
  equivalence (e.g. Bologna cycle, ENIC-NARIC/WES norms) resolves cleanly in the
  applicant's favor. No conditional steps.
- **3 — Conditionally eligible.** Eligible *if* a verifiable condition is met
  (credential evaluation pending, a bridging requirement, a master's-equivalence
  argument that is plausible but must be confirmed against the live call). Path
  exists and is realistic.
- **1 — Ineligible as-is.** A structural rule is unmet (e.g. a master's-required
  target with only a 3-year first-cycle degree and no recognized equivalence) and
  no near-term remediation exists.
- Type notes: **B/D** typically have looser entry rules; **A** (esp. EU PhD) often
  requires a master's or a recognized equivalence — the most common Vietnamese-BSc
  cross-border question lives here. **C**: eligibility also includes *sponsor*
  gating (nationality, return-home commitment, stage) — verify live.

## 2. Target & program / sponsor fit

Alignment to what the *named* program/sponsor actually weights. For scholarships
and external programs, ground in `external-programs.md`, then verify cutoffs and
required documents against the live official call.

- **5 — Sharp, evidenced fit.** The dossier maps directly onto the program's
  stated priorities with specifics (named themes, methods, people, or — for
  sponsors — mission/return-home/mobility goals) that only this applicant could
  write. Every required document type is present and on-spec.
- **3 — Generic-but-plausible fit.** Right field and credible match, but stated in
  terms that would apply to many similar programs; mission/priority language is
  present but not deeply personalized. Minor document gaps.
- **1 — Misaligned.** Target chosen by rank/convenience; the dossier ignores the
  program's stated priorities or the sponsor's mission, or required documents are
  missing/wrong-type.
- Type notes: **C** raises the bar hardest — a scholarship essay that reuses a
  generic SOP and ignores sponsor mission is a Critical failure, not a 3.

## 3. Academic record & preparation

Transcript rigor, trajectory, and relevant coursework — interpreted against the
institution and grading scale, never a hardcoded GPA cutoff.

- **5 — Strong and rigorous.** Top-of-cohort performance in demanding,
  *relevant* coursework; upward or sustained-high trajectory; advanced/graduate
  courses or independent study beyond requirements. Grade context (scale, rank)
  makes the strength legible to a foreign committee.
- **3 — Solid, uneven, or unexplained.** Good overall record but with soft spots
  in key prerequisites, a dip without context, or a strong GPA whose scale/rank is
  not contextualized for the target country.
- **1 — Weak or unprepared.** Persistent low performance in core prerequisites, or
  a record that does not demonstrate readiness for the target's coursework, with
  no remediating evidence.
- Type notes: **B** weights this highest (preparation is the master's gate). **A/C**
  read trajectory and rigor over raw average. **D** cares mainly about the
  prerequisites for the specific lab's work.

## 4. Research potential & output

Evidence of independent research contribution. **Rate publications by venue
quality using `venue-quality.md`, not raw counts.** One first-author paper at an
A*/A venue with a clear independent contribution outweighs several weak-venue or
non-archival entries.

- **5 — Demonstrated independent contribution.** ≥1–2 first-author (or clearly
  lead) papers at A*/A venues *or* an equivalently substantive artifact (a
  research-grade system, a result others build on) with evidence of intellectual
  ownership — the applicant drove the question, not just the implementation.
- **3 — Real involvement, supporting role.** Co-authored work, workshop/poster
  output, or a solid thesis where the applicant contributed meaningfully but
  ownership of the core idea is unclear or shared; venues are mid-tier (B/C) or
  non-archival.
- **1 — Coursework only.** Class projects, tutorials, or reproductions with no
  intellectual ownership and no external validation of research capability.
- Type notes: **A** weights this highest. **C** values it but trades against
  Dimension 9. **B** treats potential (not a publication record) as sufficient.
  **D** accepts strong *trajectory* (one promising project + relevant skills).

## 5. Technical & methodological depth

Command of the methods, tooling, and rigor appropriate to the field and target —
shown through artifacts, not asserted.

- **5 — Deep and demonstrated.** Evidence of mastery appropriate to the field
  (e.g. nontrivial derivations, a from-scratch implementation, rigorous empirical
  protocol, released reproducible code) at a level that would let the applicant
  contribute on day one.
- **3 — Competent, applied.** Can use standard methods/tools correctly on guided
  problems; depth is breadth-first or library-level rather than foundational.
- **1 — Surface.** Tool names appear but no artifact demonstrates real command;
  claims of skill are unsupported.
- Type notes: **D** weights this highest (immediate contribution). **A** expects
  depth to underwrite the research claims in Dimension 4.

## 6. Statement / research narrative quality

Clarity of research identity and credible future direction in the SOP/research
statement. (Prose-level editing belongs to `apply-sop-writer`; here, score the
*substance*.)

- **5 — Coherent research identity.** A specific, credible research direction that
  follows from the applicant's record and connects concretely to the target; the
  reader finishes knowing what kind of researcher this is and what they want to
  pursue, with specifics not slogans.
- **3 — Competent but generic.** Readable and organized, but the direction is
  broad ("I am passionate about AI"), fit is stated generically, and the future
  plan could belong to many applicants.
- **1 — Unfocused or mismatched.** No discernible direction, contradicts the CV,
  or is a personal-history essay with no research thesis.
- Type notes: **C** also requires the statement to carry the sponsor's
  mission/return-home logic credibly, not as an afterthought.

## 7. Letters of recommendation

Strength, relevance, and coverage of recommenders relative to the target. Score on
*expected* signal from who they are and what they can attest, not the unseen text.

- **5 — Strong, research-relevant, well-covered.** Recommenders who supervised the
  applicant's research can speak first-hand to research ability, with at least one
  recognized in the field; together they cover research, technical, and
  growth dimensions for the target.
- **3 — Mixed or thin coverage.** A credible research letter plus course-only or
  managerial letters; or strong people who know the applicant only loosely. Some
  required dimension uncovered.
- **1 — Weak or wrong-type.** Manager-only / character-only letters for a research
  target, or recommenders who cannot speak to the abilities the target cares about.
- Type notes: **A/C** weight research letters most. **D** values a letter from
  someone who can vouch for hands-on competence in the lab's area.

## 8. Faculty / lab fit

Concreteness of match to named people or groups. **Fetch this live per run**
(Scholar / DBLP / lab page); there is no cached professor file by design.

- **5 — Specific, current, two-way.** Named faculty whose *recent* (not decade-old)
  work the applicant engages with substantively, with a plausible reason the lab
  would want this applicant; evidence the direction is active (recent papers,
  funding, students).
- **3 — Plausible but shallow.** Named people in the right area, but the connection
  is to old/peripheral work or stated at the level of shared keywords.
- **1 — Absent or stale.** No named fit, or fit asserted to a professor who has
  moved on from that line of work.
- Type notes: **D/A** weight this most. **B/C** need program-level fit; named-PI fit
  is a bonus unless the program requires a pre-arranged supervisor.

## 9. Leadership, service & impact

Especially weighted for scholarships — includes return-home / development-impact /
mobility signals where the *sponsor requires them* (verify live).

- **5 — Sustained, evidenced impact.** Clear leadership or service with outcomes
  (built/ran something, mentored, contributed to community/development) that maps
  onto the sponsor's mission; for return-home sponsors, a concrete, credible
  plan tied to the applicant's record.
- **3 — Some involvement.** Participation and minor roles; mission/return-home
  intent stated but generic or not backed by prior action.
- **1 — Absent or pro-forma.** No service/leadership evidence, or a mission
  statement that contradicts the applicant's stated plans.
- Type notes: **C** weights this highest (often co-equal with excellence). **A/B/D**
  treat it as a positive secondary signal, not a gate.

## 10. Dossier coherence

Does the package tell one consistent story? Work `assets/coherence-checklist.md`.

- **5 — One story.** The SOP's lead narrative is visible and prominent in the CV;
  every SOP claim is CV-backed; the proposal matches the target; transcript
  weaknesses are addressed; all documents sound like the same applicant.
- **3 — Mostly aligned.** Broadly consistent but with loose ends — a CV strength
  unused in the SOP, an SOP claim only weakly evidenced, or uneven emphasis.
- **1 — Fragmented.** Documents imply different applicants/theses, key claims are
  unsupported across documents, or the proposal does not match the target.

## 11. Risk & readiness

Red flags and submission-readiness. Classify findings Critical / Major / Minor
using `failure-patterns.md`.

- **5 — Submission-ready.** No Critical or Major flags; only cosmetic Minor items;
  all live-verify items (limits, deadlines, required docs) checked and satisfied.
- **3 — Fixable gaps.** No Criticals, but ≥1 Major flag (e.g. wrong-type letter,
  unaddressed transcript anomaly) that must be resolved before submission.
- **1 — Not ready.** ≥1 Critical flag (e.g. ineligible as-is, scholarship essay
  ignores sponsor mission, missing required document) blocking submission.
