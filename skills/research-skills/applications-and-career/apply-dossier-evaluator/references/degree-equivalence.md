# Degree equivalence & eligibility frameworks

Eligibility logic the scorecard's Dimension 1 (gate) needs for EU / cross-border
targets. This file carries **stable recognition frameworks only** — it does NOT
decide any individual case. Recognition is always made by the receiving
institution and (where required) a credential evaluator; treat everything here as
the structure for asking the right question, then **verify the specific case
against the live program rules and an official evaluator**.

> **Do not invent eligibility verdicts.** The frameworks below are stable; the
> outcome for a given applicant/program is not. Where a verdict is needed, output
> the verify-live step, not a guess.

## The three frameworks

- **Bologna / EHEA** (<https://ehea.info>) — the structural model most EU PhD entry
  rules are written against (cycles, ECTS).
- **ENIC-NARIC** (<https://enic-naric.net>) — the official national recognition
  centres; the authority for how a foreign qualification compares in a given
  country.
- **WES** (<https://wes.org>) — a widely-accepted course-by-course/credential
  evaluator, common for US (and some other) applications.

## Bologna / EHEA three-cycle structure

- **First cycle** — bachelor, typically 180–240 ECTS.
- **Second cycle** — master, typically 90–120 ECTS.
- **Third cycle** — doctorate.

Key structural fact: many EU PhD programs require **completion of the second cycle
(a master's)** for entry. A first-cycle degree alone is often insufficient *unless*
the program explicitly admits strong bachelor's holders or recognizes an
equivalence. (ECTS ranges are the stable Bologna design; a specific program's
required credits/cycle is **program-specific — verify live**.)

## ENIC-NARIC recognition network

- Recognition of a foreign degree is a **national/institutional decision**, routed
  through the destination country's ENIC-NARIC centre and/or the admitting
  university.
- The same foreign degree can be recognized differently in different countries.
- Use ENIC-NARIC as the authority for "how is this qualification viewed *here*",
  not a global verdict. **Verify per destination country.**

## WES and credential evaluation norms

- WES (and peer evaluators) produce a course-by-course or document-by-document
  equivalence statement used by many institutions, especially in the US.
- A WES evaluation is an **input** to the admitting institution's decision, not the
  decision itself.
- Processing requires official transcripts and time; flag it as a **conditional
  step** (Dimension 1 = "conditionally eligible") when results are pending.

## The recurring case: 4-year Vietnamese BSc → master's-required EU PhD

Structure for reasoning (NOT a verdict):

- A 4-year Vietnamese BSc is commonly assessed as a **first-cycle (bachelor-level)**
  qualification under Bologna comparison — its extra year does **not** automatically
  make it second-cycle (master's) equivalent.
- Therefore, for an EU PhD that **requires a master's**, a 4-year BSc alone is often
  **not sufficient on its own** — eligibility usually hinges on one of:
  1. the program explicitly admitting strong bachelor's holders, or
  2. an ENIC-NARIC / institutional recognition that treats the degree as
     master's-equivalent (uncommon for a 4-year bachelor — must be confirmed), or
  3. completing a master's first.
- Score Dimension 1 accordingly: **Ineligible as-is** if the target hard-requires a
  master's and none of (1)–(3) applies; **Conditional** if a recognition or
  program exception is plausible but unconfirmed; **Eligible** only when the
  program's own rules clearly admit the applicant. **Always verify against the
  specific program + the destination ENIC-NARIC centre.**

## US vs EU credit & entry expectations

- **US PhD:** typically admits **directly from a bachelor's** (the PhD includes
  coursework), so a 4-year BSc is usually structurally eligible; credentials are
  often run through WES. Selectivity, not cycle structure, is the constraint.
- **EU PhD:** more often **master's-entry** and research-only, so the cycle
  question above is live.
- Credit systems differ (US semester/quarter credits vs. ECTS); do not convert by a
  fixed ratio — let the evaluator/institution map them. **Verify both the entry
  level and the credit expectation against the specific program.**

## Verify-live checklist

- [ ] Does the target program **require a master's**, or admit strong bachelor's
      holders? (read the official entry requirements)
- [ ] How does the **destination country's ENIC-NARIC centre** view a 4-year
      Vietnamese BSc?
- [ ] Is a **credential evaluation** (WES or equivalent) required, and is it pending?
- [ ] Any **bridging / qualifying** requirements stated by the program?
- [ ] For sponsors: does the **scholarship** add its own degree/stage eligibility
      rules on top of the program's? (see `external-programs.md`)
