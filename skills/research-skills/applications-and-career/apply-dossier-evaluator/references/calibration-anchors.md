# Calibration anchors — worked example dossiers

Use these fixed reference dossiers to keep the scale stable across runs: place the
dossier you are scoring next to the closest anchors and check your total lands in
the same tier. Anchors use the **top-2% / next-8%** tiering style
(alvinwan.com/how-phd-admissions-committees-assess-applications) and reviewer
mechanics from the "demystifying CS PhD admissions" reviewer notes
(roars.dev/phd-cs-us/demystify.pdf).

> **These examples are SYNTHETIC**, constructed to illustrate the tiers — not real
> applicants and not outcome data. Replace or supplement with real anonymized
> evaluated dossiers when available; real anchors calibrate the scale better.
> Scores reference the 1–5 anchors in `rubric-descriptors.md` and the adaptive
> weights in `SKILL.md`. Field context is ML/AI for concreteness; transpose to the
> applicant's field.

## Tiers

- **Top (~top 2%)** — clear admit/funding signal at competitive targets.
- **Strong (~next 8%)** — competitive; specific gaps to close.
- **Plausible** — viable at well-matched targets; material gaps.
- **Developing** — not yet competitive at the stated target.

## Type A — Research PhD

### A1 — Top (~top 2%)
**Snapshot:** Top-of-cohort BSc+MSc at a strong national university; 2 first-author
papers (one NeurIPS, one ICML workshop), released reproducible code; MSc thesis
extended into the NeurIPS paper; SOP names 3 faculty whose *recent* work it engages
substantively; two research-supervisor letters, one field-recognized.
**Scores (D1–D11):** 5 (eligible) · 4 · 5 · 5 · 4 · 5 · 5 · 5 · 3 · 5 · 5.
**Why Top:** D4=5 (first-author A* with ownership), D8=5 (specific current fit),
coherent story (D10=5), no flags (D11=5). The only non-5s are leadership (not a
PhD gate) and breadth of technical depth. Clear admit signal.

### A2 — Plausible
**Snapshot:** Good GPA, one co-authored workshop paper (middle author), strong
implementation skills but no first-author result; SOP states a broad interest in
"deep learning" and names a department but no specific faculty fit; one research
letter + two course letters.
**Scores:** 5 · 3 · 4 · 2 · 4 · 3 · 3 · 2 · 2 · 3 · 3.
**Why Plausible:** D4=2 (involvement, no ownership), D8=2 (no specific fit), D6=3
(generic direction). Viable at well-matched/less-competitive programs; the
highest-leverage fix is a first-author result and a specific fit.

## Type B — Research / coursework Master's

### B1 — Strong (~next 8%)
**Snapshot:** Excellent transcript with graduate-level coursework taken early,
strong upward trajectory, one solid undergraduate thesis (no publication), good
recommenders who can speak to preparation; SOP frames the master's as a deliberate
step toward a research direction.
**Scores:** 5 · 4 · 5 · 3 · 4 · 4 · 4 · 2 · 3 · 4 · 5.
**Why Strong:** B weights **academic record (D3=5)** and preparation highest;
research output (D4=3) is sufficient at this target because *potential*, not a
publication record, is the bar. Competitive for research master's admission.

### B2 — Developing
**Snapshot:** Average record with soft grades in core prerequisites, no research
exposure, SOP is a personal-history essay with no research thesis, course-only
letters.
**Scores:** 5 · 2 · 2 · 1 · 2 · 1 · 2 · 1 · 2 · 2 · 3.
**Why Developing:** D3=2 (weak prerequisites), D4=1 (coursework only), D6=1 (no
thesis). Not yet competitive; fixes are prerequisite remediation and any genuine
research exposure before applying.

## Type C — Scholarship / Fellowship

*(Primary target — three anchors. Sponsor mission/return-home is weighted highest.)*

### C1 — Top (~top 2%)
**Snapshot:** Strong academic record + one first-author A-venue paper; *and* a
sustained, evidenced record of community/development leadership (founded a student
research group, mentored juniors, ran outreach) with a concrete, credible
return-home plan tied to that record; essays are tailored to the sponsor's stated
mission, not a reused SOP; all required documents present and on-spec.
**Scores:** 5 · 5 · 5 · 4 · 4 · 5 · 4 · 3 · 5 · 5 · 5.
**Why Top:** D9=5 and D2=5 (mission fit) carry the most weight for C, and excellence
(D3/D4) backs them. Coherent, no flags. Strong funding signal.

### C2 — Strong (~next 8%)
**Snapshot:** Excellent academics and a real research project, but leadership is
participation-level and the return-home/mission language is stated genuinely yet
generically; essays are tailored but lean academic.
**Scores:** 5 · 3 · 5 · 4 · 4 · 4 · 4 · 3 · 3 · 4 · 4.
**Why Strong:** excellence is there, but D9=3 and D2=3 (generic mission framing)
keep it below Top for a mission-driven sponsor. Highest-leverage fix: evidence the
mission with prior action, not intent.

### C3 — Developing (Critical flag)
**Snapshot:** Good academics, but the scholarship essay is a lightly-edited reuse of
a generic PhD SOP that never addresses the sponsor's mission or return-home
expectation.
**Scores:** 5 · 1 · 4 · 3 · 3 · 2 · 3 · 2 · 1 · 2 · 1.
**Why Developing:** D2=1 and D11=1 — "scholarship essay reuses generic SOP, ignores
sponsor mission" is a **Critical** failure (see `failure-patterns.md`), capping the
dossier regardless of solid academics. Must rewrite essays to the sponsor.

## Type D — Research internship / pre-doc / RA

### D1 — Strong (~next 8%)
**Snapshot:** Solid student with strong, *demonstrated* technical depth (released
code, a from-scratch implementation in the lab's exact area), a specific named-PI
fit to recent lab work, and a letter from someone who can vouch for hands-on
competence; modest publication record.
**Scores:** 5 · 4 · 4 · 3 · 5 · 4 · 4 · 5 · 2 · 4 · 5.
**Why Strong:** D weights **technical depth (D5=5)** and **named fit (D8=5)** highest
— immediate contribution matters more than a publication record (D4=3 is fine).
Competitive for a research placement.

### D2 — Plausible
**Snapshot:** Capable generalist, library-level skills but no artifact showing deep
command, fit stated at keyword level to the lab, no hands-on letter.
**Scores:** 5 · 3 · 4 · 2 · 3 · 3 · 2 · 3 · 2 · 3 · 4.
**Why Plausible:** D5=3 and D8=3 leave the immediate-contribution case unproven.
Highest-leverage fix: a concrete artifact in the lab's area and a sharper fit.
