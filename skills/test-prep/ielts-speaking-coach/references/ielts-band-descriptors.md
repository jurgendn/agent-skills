# IELTS Speaking Band Descriptors (reference)

Scoring reference for `ielts-speaking-coach`. Read the relevant criterion
**before** assigning a band so scoring stays anchored to the public criteria
rather than to a remembered impression.

> **Status & attribution.** This is a faithful *paraphrase* of the publicly
> released IELTS band descriptors, condensed to bands 5–9 (the range most
> candidates work in). It is a working aid, **not** the official wording. For
> the verbatim descriptors and bands below 5, use the official sources — they
> are the authority if anything here disagrees:
> - Speaking: <https://www.ielts.org/-/media/pdfs/speaking-band-descriptors.ashx>
> - Overview of criteria: <https://ielts.org/news-and-insights/ielts-writing-band-descriptors-and-key-assessment-criteria>

Speaking is scored on **four equally weighted criteria (25% each)**; the four
band scores are averaged and rounded to the nearest half band.

---

## Speaking

Criteria: **Fluency & Coherence · Lexical Resource · Grammatical Range & Accuracy · Pronunciation**

### Fluency & Coherence
- **9** — Speaks fluently with only rare repetition/self-correction; hesitation is content-related, not language-search; coherent, fully appropriate cohesion.
- **8** — Fluent with only occasional repetition/self-correction; hesitation usually content-related; develops topics coherently and appropriately.
- **7** — Speaks at length without noticeable effort; some language-related hesitation or loss of coherence; flexible use of connectives/discourse markers.
- **6** — Willing to speak at length, though coherence may be lost through repetition, self-correction, or hesitation; uses a range of connectives but not always appropriately.
- **5** — Usually maintains flow but relies on repetition/self-correction/slow speech; may over-use certain connectives; produces simple speech fluently but loses fluency in complex communication.

### Lexical Resource
- **9** — Full flexibility and precise use in all contexts; idiomatic language used naturally and accurately.
- **8** — Wide resource used readily and flexibly to convey precise meaning; effective use of less common and idiomatic items with occasional inaccuracy; effective paraphrase.
- **7** — Flexible vocabulary to discuss a variety of topics; some less common and idiomatic items with some awareness of style/collocation; effective paraphrase as needed.
- **6** — Wide enough to discuss topics at length and make meaning clear despite inappropriacies; generally able to paraphrase successfully.
- **5** — Manages to talk about familiar and unfamiliar topics but uses vocabulary with limited flexibility; attempts paraphrase with mixed success.

### Grammatical Range & Accuracy
- **9** — Full range used naturally and accurately; very rare minor slips like those of a native speaker.
- **8** — Wide range of structures flexibly; majority error-free; occasional inappropriacies/basic non-systematic errors.
- **7** — A range of complex structures with some flexibility; frequent error-free sentences though some grammatical mistakes persist.
- **6** — A mix of short and complex forms but with limited flexibility; frequent errors in complex structures, though these rarely impede communication.
- **5** — Basic forms with reasonable accuracy; limited range of complex structures, usually with errors that may cause some difficulty.

### Pronunciation
- **9** — Full range of features used precisely and subtly; effortless to understand.
- **8** — Wide range of features; sustains flexible use with only occasional lapses; easy to understand, L1 accent has minimal effect.
- **7** — Shows all the positive features of band 6 and some, but not all, of band 8; generally easy to understand throughout.
- **6** — A range of features with mixed control; some effective use but not sustained; generally understood throughout, though mispronunciation of individual words/sounds reduces clarity at times.
- **5** — Shows all the positive features of band 4 and some of band 6; can generally be understood but mispronunciation causes lapses.

## How to score (apply consistently)

1. Score **each of the four criteria independently** — don't let one strong dimension inflate the others.
2. For a criterion, find the band whose description the response **best fits as a whole**; if it sits between two, award the half band.
3. Average the four (round to the nearest half band). State the four sub-scores, not just the overall — the breakdown is where actionable feedback lives.
4. Tie every band you give to **specific evidence** in the response (quote the phrase, the error, the hesitation pattern), then name the single highest-leverage move to the next band.
