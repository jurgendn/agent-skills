# GRE Analytical Writing — Issue task score levels (0–6)

Compact paraphrase of the ETS scoring guide for "Analyze an Issue," for anchoring score estimates. For the authoritative wording, consult the official ETS scoring guide and published scored sample essays: https://www.ets.org/gre (Analytical Writing section). Final scores are the average of a human rater and ETS's automated scorer, reported in half-point increments.

| Score | Position & insight | Development | Organization | Language |
|-------|--------------------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **6 — Outstanding** | Insightful, in-depth position on the issue as instructed; complexities acknowledged | Compelling reasons and persuasive, apt examples; ideas fully developed and logically connected | Focused and well organized; connections are seamless | Precise vocabulary, varied sentences, superior fluency; errors rare and trivial |
| **5 — Strong** | Thoughtful, generally in-depth position per the instructions | Logically sound reasons and well-chosen examples; well developed | Focused and generally well organized; clear transitions | Good control, sentence variety, apt vocabulary; minor errors only |
| **4 — Adequate** | Clear position per the instructions, but limited depth or nuance | Relevant reasons and examples, adequately but unevenly developed | Adequate organization; grouping and transitions may be mechanical | Sufficient clarity and control; some errors, meaning not obscured |
| **3 — Limited** | Position vague, or instructions only partially followed | Reasons/examples thin, tangential, or poorly reasoned | Poorly focused or poorly organized | Limited control; errors sometimes obscure meaning |
| **2 — Seriously flawed** | Unclear or unsupported position; little grasp of the task | Few relevant reasons or examples; severely underdeveloped | Disorganized | Frequent serious errors that often obscure meaning |
| **1 — Fundamentally deficient** | Little or no evidence of understanding the issue | Little or no development | Little or no organization | Pervasive errors; largely incoherent |
| **0** | Off-topic, not in English, a copy of the prompt, or otherwise unscorable | | | |

Half-point scores sit between adjacent descriptors (e.g., 4.5 = clearly above Adequate, not consistently Strong).

## Fast anchoring heuristics

- **The 3/4 line** is task compliance + relevance: a clear on-task position with relevant, if shallow, support is a 4.
- **The 4/5 line** is development quality: specific, examined examples and logically *connected* (not just listed) reasons.
- **The 5/6 line** is insight: complexity handled convincingly — limits of the position marked, strongest objection genuinely engaged — plus notably fluent prose.
- Language errors drag the score only when frequent or meaning-obscuring; polish alone never lifts an underdeveloped essay above 4.
